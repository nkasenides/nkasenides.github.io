About me

I am a research associate at the University of Central Lancashire and a PhD student specializing in Software Engineering, Cloud computing and Distributed Systems.

My PhD research topic, titled "Models, Methods and Tools for developing MMOG back-ends using Commodity Cloud Platforms", aims to bring multiplayer online games a step closer to being hosted on commodity clouds by enabling them to run on the Platform as a Service layer of cloud computing. Click here to learn more. My research interests include a variety of fields which are predominantly related to software engineering.

I have attained a BSc in Computing from UCLan Cyprus, with emphasis in Software Engineering and have worked as a part-time web developer during my studies. As a software engineer, I have a broad range of interests and skills including Android and web app development, software development tools, enterprise applications and more. I also have a limited amount of teaching experience as I have create and deliver worksheets for a BSc module at UCLan Cyprus.

In my free time, I am usually at the gym, playing basketball or playing Ghost Recon: Wildlands on PC as hf-november and uploading content to my YouTube channel (Team HF). I also like listening to trance music and create mixes which I upload to my Mixcloud channel. When I get bored, I usually work on one of my own projects.
